Circle

A circle is a closed geometric shape in which all points are at the same distance from the center. It is one of the most important shapes in mathematics and geometry. Circles are widely used in science, engineering, architecture, and everyday life.
Circles can be seen in wheels, clocks, coins, rings, and plates. In transportation, circular wheels help vehicles move smoothly and efficiently. Engineers use circles in machines, gears, and rotating systems.
In architecture, circles are used in domes, arches, and decorative structures. Artists also use circular designs to create beautiful patterns and artwork.
Circles are important in astronomy because planets and moons move in paths related to circular motion. Scientists use circles to study rotation and movement. In technology, circles are used in cameras, speakers, and electronic devices.
Sports also use circles in many games such as basketball, cricket, and athletics. Circular tracks and balls are common examples.
The study of circles helps improve understanding of geometry, measurement, and motion. Therefore, circles play a major role in mathematics and real life applications.
