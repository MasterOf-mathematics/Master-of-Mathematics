Continuity and Differentiability

Continuity and differentiability are important concepts in mathematics that help in understanding the behavior of functions and graphs. These concepts are widely used in calculus, physics, engineering, and economics.
A function is called continuous when its graph can be drawn without lifting the pen. Continuity helps in studying smooth changes in values. Differentiability is related to finding derivatives and understanding the rate of change.
These concepts are very useful in physics for studying motion, speed, and acceleration. Engineers use continuity and differentiability while designing machines, bridges, and systems. Economists use them to study market growth and profit changes.
In computer science, continuity and differentiability are used in graphics, animations, and artificial intelligence. Scientists also use these concepts in weather forecasting and population studies.
Continuity and differentiability help students understand graphs and functions clearly. They improve logical thinking and problem solving abilities.
