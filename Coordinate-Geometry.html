Coordinate Geometry

Coordinate geometry is a branch of mathematics that studies geometric shapes using coordinates and graphs. It connects algebra with geometry and helps solve problems related to points, lines, curves, and distances.
Coordinate geometry is widely used in engineering, architecture, navigation, and computer graphics. Engineers use it while designing roads, bridges, and buildings. Architects use coordinate geometry for creating maps and layouts.
In navigation, coordinate geometry helps determine location and direction. Pilots and sailors use coordinates to travel safely. In computer graphics and video games, coordinate geometry helps create realistic images and movements.
Scientists use coordinate geometry in astronomy to study the position of stars and planets. It is also useful in robotics and machine design.
Coordinate geometry improves analytical thinking and helps students understand graphs and measurements clearly. It is one of the most practical branches of mathematics.
