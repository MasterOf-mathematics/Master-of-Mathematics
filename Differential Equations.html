Differential Equations

Differential equation is a branch of mathematics that studies equations involving rates of change. It is widely used in science, engineering, economics, biology, and physics. Differential equations help describe real life situations mathematically.
In physics, differential equations are used to study motion, electricity, heat, and waves. Engineers use them while designing machines, circuits, and mechanical systems. In economics, differential equations help study population growth, investment, and market behavior.
Differential equations are also important in biology and medicine. Scientists use them to study disease spread, blood flow, and population changes. Environmental scientists use them to analyze climate and pollution.
In technology and computer science, differential equations are useful in simulations, robotics, and artificial intelligence. They help create accurate mathematical models for complex systems.
Overall, differential equations are very important because they connect mathematics with practical and scientific applications.
