Introduction of Trigonometry

Trigonometry is a branch of mathematics that studies the relationship between angles and sides of triangles. It is widely used in science, engineering, navigation, and astronomy. Trigonometry helps measure heights, distances, and directions accurately.
The introduction of trigonometry begins with understanding angles, triangles, and ratios. Ancient mathematicians developed trigonometry to study astronomy and construction work. Today, it is one of the most important topics in mathematics.
In engineering and architecture, trigonometry is used to design buildings, bridges, and roads. Pilots and sailors use trigonometry for navigation and direction finding. In astronomy, scientists use it to measure the distance between stars and planets.
Trigonometry is also useful in computer graphics, animations, and sound engineering. It helps create realistic motion and visual effects.
Overall, trigonometry is important because it connects mathematics with practical applications in daily life and technology.
