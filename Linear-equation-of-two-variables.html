Linear Equation of Two Variables

A linear equation of two variables is an equation involving two unknown quantities. These equations are represented by straight lines on graphs and are important in mathematics and real life applications.
Linear equations of two variables are used in business, economics, and science. They help solve problems involving cost, profit, distance, and time. Businesses use them for budgeting and planning.
In coordinate geometry, linear equations help study graphs and intersections. Engineers use them in designing systems and structures. Economists use them to analyze supply and demand relationships.
Linear equations also help students understand relationships between quantities. They improve logical reasoning and problem solving skills.
Overall, linear equations of two variables are useful for solving practical and mathematical problems.
